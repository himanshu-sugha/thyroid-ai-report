Evolution of Techniques

A summary of the prevailing trends and findings from over a decade of research.

Early 2010s: Foundational Models

Initial research heavily relied on foundational models like SVM, Naive Bayes, and basic Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) to establish the viability of ML for thyroid diagnosis on structured datasets like the UCI repository.

Mid-Late 2010s: Rise of Ensembles

The focus shifted to more robust ensemble methods. Random Forest and Gradient Boosting became dominant, consistently achieving higher accuracy by combining multiple decision trees and addressing overfitting.

2020-Present: Deep Learning Era

Deep Learning, especially CNNs, revolutionized image-based diagnosis (ultrasound, scintigraphy). For tabular data, advanced techniques like stacking ensembles and explainable AI (XAI) are now at the forefront.
