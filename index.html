Evolution of Techniques

A summary of the prevailing trends and findings from over a decade of research.

Early 2010s: Foundational Models & Feature Engineering

Initial research heavily focused on SVMs and ANNs, with significant effort in feature engineering—extracting textural features from ultrasound, using fuzzy logic for noise resistance, and incorporating new modalities like elastography.

Late 2010s: Rise of Deep Learning

The focus shifted dramatically to Deep Learning. Landmark studies applied powerful CNNs (e.g., Inception-v3) to large ultrasound datasets, proving models could exceed radiologist performance and highlighting the need for external validation to ensure generalizability.

2020s: Advanced Architectures & Clinical Integration

Current research leverages sophisticated models like Vision Transformers (Swin) and advanced ensembles (Stacking, LightGBM). The focus has expanded beyond diagnosis to personalization (dosage prediction), risk stratification (recurrence), and interpretability (SHAP).
