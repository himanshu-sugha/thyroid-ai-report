Evolution of Techniques

A summary of the prevailing trends and findings from over a decade of research.

Early 2010s: Foundational Models

Initial research heavily relied on foundational models like SVM, Naive Bayes, and basic Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) to establish the viability of ML for thyroid diagnosis on structured datasets and ultrasound images.

Mid-Late 2010s: Rise of Ensembles & Deep Learning

The focus shifted to more robust ensemble methods like Random Forest and Gradient Boosting for clinical data. Simultaneously, Deep Learning with CNNs (e.g., Inception-v3) began to revolutionize image-based diagnosis.

2020-Present: Advanced Architectures & XAI

Modern research leverages advanced architectures (e.g., Vision Transformers, multi-channel CNNs) and focuses on clinical utility through generalizability testing and explainable AI (XAI) for model transparency.
